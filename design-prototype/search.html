The pipelineWhat runs on your box

Every model runs inside your jurisdiction.

Embedding, transcription, OCR, translation, named-entity extraction — all ship as containerised models that run on CPU or your own GPU. Nothing crosses your perimeter. The witnesses in your database are not product data for somebody else's frontier lab.

01 · STT

Whisper-large-v3 on-prem

Transcription for audio and video. 47 languages, speaker diarisation, timestamp-anchored captions sealed with the parent exhibit.

02 · OCR

docTR + handwriting adapter

Document and handwritten page OCR. Per-character confidence scores preserved; low-confidence ranges are flagged to the analyst rather than silently corrected.

03 · EMB

BGE-M3 multilingual

Cross-lingual dense embeddings for semantic search. A Ukrainian query matches Russian, English, and French content at the same ranking call.

04 · NER

GLiNER — custom labels

Names, places, call-signs, vehicle IDs, weapon types. Operators train per-case label sets without shipping data anywhere — fine-tunes stay on the box.

05 · TRANS

NLLB-200 · pivot for rare pairs

Neural translation, 200 languages. Translations are rendered as annotations — never silently replace source text in the sealed exhibit.

ProvenanceEvery answer, cited

An answer you can file.

Every result carries the sealed exhibit ID, the hash, the chain index, and the custody state. An investigator can export a cross-exhibit finding as a PDF that defence counsel's clerk validator can verify — without ever touching VaultKeeper's servers.

Hash-bound

Every hit cites a sealed row

Search results are not a separate index. They reference the custody chain's own row IDs. If the chain is broken, the search result is marked broken, too.

Offline

Exports verify without us

A finding exported from search becomes a ZIP with the cited exhibit slices and a 240-line validator. Defence counsel runs it on their laptop. They don't need VaultKeeper to check your work.

Zero telemetry

Queries never leave the perimeter

Search logs live in the same sealed ledger as ingest. They are not pushed to VaultKeeper — we cannot see them, and neither can anyone who gains access to our infrastructure.
