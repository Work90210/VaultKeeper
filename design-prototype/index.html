What we replace

Built for the work that cannot be paused by a sanction.

Legacy e-discovery tools run on clouds your jurisdiction doesn't control. A political shift in Washington should not end a war-crimes investigation in The Hague. VaultKeeper is the sovereign alternative — built ground-up for institutions that cannot depend on any single country to stay online.

i.

Sovereign by default

Self-host on your own infrastructure — or any EU cloud. Zero telemetry, zero outbound calls, air-gap compatible. No vendor lock-in, no Azure tether.

ii.

Provably append-only

Every custody event is hash-chained and written to an append-only log the database itself refuses to rewrite. Even a root superuser cannot alter history.

iii.

Witness-safe

AES-256-GCM application-level encryption of witness PII. Defence sees pseudonyms. Duress passphrases produce decoy vaults.

iv.

Open source, always

AGPL-3.0. Read the code, audit the crypto, fork if you have to. Your evidence survives the company that built the software.

How it works

Seizure to sentence, every link signed.

Four phases, each cryptographically bound to the next. The story of who held the evidence isn't told by a spreadsheet — it's proven by a chain of hashes a defence expert can verify offline.

Ingest

Chunked resumable uploads, client-side SHA-256, RFC 3161 timestamping at the door. If a single byte is off, the upload fails with a 409.

Seal

Append-only custody log. Each row hash-chains the previous one. PostgreSQL RLS refuses UPDATEs and DELETEs — even from superusers.

Work

Real-time CRDT collaboration, redaction editor, witness linking, corroboration scoring, inquiry logs — all writing to the same sealed chain.

Export

One-click ZIP with evidence, custody log, and hash manifest. The court's clerk verifies it with our open validator — no VaultKeeper service required.
