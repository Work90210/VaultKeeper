II. Statement of custody

This dossier constitutes the formal disclosure of forty-eight exhibits held by Eurojust, The Hague, under the case reference ICC-UKR-2024, to the defence counsel identified on the cover page, pursuant to the disclosure obligations of the International Criminal Court Office of the Prosecutor.

Each exhibit listed in §I has been in continuous custody from the moment of intake to the moment of seal. No exhibit has been modified after sealing; amendments, where present, are themselves sealed events and appear with their predecessors in the accompanying chain log.

Redactions applied to individual exhibits are enumerated in the per-exhibit redaction map (redactions/ directory of the bundle). Every redaction carries the Ed25519 signature of the analyst who applied it and the countersignature of the legal officer. No redaction is irreversible by the custodian; the original, unredacted bytes remain sealed and are released to the chamber on judicial order.

V. How to verify this dossier

1. Take the accompanying bundle (USB stick or downloaded ZIP). It contains the exhibits, the redaction maps, a manifest.json, a chain.log, and the open-source validator vk-verify.

2. On an air-gapped workstation, run ./vk-verify --bundle DISC-2026-019. The validator will recompute every hash, verify every Ed25519 signature, reconstruct the chain back to this dossier's head, and check the RFC 3161 timestamps against the public key of ts-eu-west included in the bundle.

3. If any byte of any exhibit, this dossier, the manifest, or the chain log has been altered since seal, the validator will exit FAIL and print the first divergence. If all is well, the validator exits PASS and writes a signed verified.txt receipt into the bundle directory.

The chamber, the defence, and any neutral third party are invited to run this verification independently. VaultKeeper, Eurojust, and the custodian are not required to be online; the chain speaks for itself.
