Company · Disclosure

If you find a flaw, we want it.

Our customers are tribunals and witness-protection programs. A bug in our custody engine does not mean an inconvenienced user — it can mean a case file whose chain of custody silently breaks. If you have found a vulnerability in VaultKeeper, please report it. This page is how.

How to reportThree channels

1 · Preferred: encrypted email

security@vaultkeeper.coop, encrypted to our PGP key 3F9A 12C4 8B7D 4E22 7F55 C104 A9AB 6620 44D8. Acknowledgement in 24 hours, triage in 72 hours, fix timeline communicated within 10 business days. We do not ask you to sign an NDA to report.

2 · Signal

For urgent or high-sensitivity reports, Signal username vk.security.01. Two engineers monitor it on rotation. If you need anonymity, use a burner Signal identity — we will not log or correlate metadata.

3 · Paper

If digital channels feel unsafe, send a sealed envelope to Security, VaultKeeper Coöperatief U.A., Prinsengracht 54, 1015 DV Amsterdam. Include a return Signal handle or a PGP key on paper. Yes, we do receive these.

What to include

A clear reproducer, affected version(s), impact you believe it has, and — if you want public credit — the name or handle you want listed in the advisory. You do not need to include a suggested fix; you do not need to prove exploitability beyond a reasonable test case.

The bountyWhat we pay

Modest, predictable, and we pay within 30 days.

The VaultKeeper bounty is not Silicon-Valley scale. We are a cooperative. But every bounty we have committed to, we have paid — and we publish the ledger. Severity is scored against impact on a tribunal's custody chain, not against generic CVSS.

Safe harbourOur promise to researchers

We will not take legal action against good-faith research

If you are acting in good faith — not exfiltrating data, not targeting live customer deployments, not harming availability — we will not pursue civil or criminal action against you. This commitment applies against the Dutch Wet Computercriminaliteit, the US CFAA, and any equivalent under which we might otherwise have standing. We do not sue researchers. We hire them.

Researchers acting under this safe harbour may test against our public demo instance at demo.vaultkeeper.coop, or a self-hosted instance you have set up yourself. Do not test against customer instances; if you believe a customer instance is vulnerable, tell us and we'll coordinate.

Public disclosure timeline

We commit to patching critical issues within 30 days of a validated report, high-severity within 60, medium within 90. Public advisories are published after a patch is deployed to all supported customers, typically 14 days later. You are free to publish your own write-up after our advisory; we will link to it in the security page.
