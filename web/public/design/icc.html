The architectureNot a cluster. A mesh.

Three sites. Independent authority. One sealed ledger.

At ICC-scale, a single-site deployment is a political liability. The Sovereign tier deploys as three independent instances in three jurisdictions, each holding full signing authority, mutually federating via VKE1. Loss of any one site — legal, physical, or cryptographic — does not stop operations.

01 · PRIMARY

Institutional HQ

The main operating site — typically the institution's own datacentre in The Hague, Geneva, or Arusha. Full write authority. HSM-backed.

02 · SOVEREIGN

State-sponsored peer

A second instance in a jurisdiction the primary institution selects — a friendly state agency or allied tribunal. Independent HSM, independent key-ceremony ritual.

03 · ARCHIVAL

Air-gapped mirror

A third, fully offline mirror — updated via sealed-drive courier. Purpose-built for catastrophic recovery: if the first two sites are both compromised, this one is the court of record.

What shipsIncluded in Sovereign

The engagement looks nothing like a SaaS contract.

01

Dedicated deployment team

Named, resident engineers for 12 months. Two VaultKeeper principals embed with your registrar and head of IT through migration, key ceremony, and the first major exhibit cycle.

02

Source escrow

A copy of the source tree sealed with a Dutch notary. If VaultKeeper coöp ceases to exist, your registrar receives the escrow on 30 days' notice. You have our source whether or not we do.

03

Annual third-party audit

NCC Group or Cure53, funded by us, scope set by you. Full report goes to you unredacted; summary goes public. Latest audit is linked on our security page.

04

Quarterly key ceremony

HSM key rotations at your site, witnessed by your custodians. Old keys are kept sealed in a tamper-evident bag under your custody — never ours.

05

Formal verification of the custody engine

The append-only ledger module is F*-verified. We publish proofs of its critical invariants — monotonicity, signature completeness, merge associativity.
