The custody log is not a table with a timestamp column. It is an append-only cryptographic structure, hash-chained and witness-signed, that the database engine itself refuses to modify — even if ordered to by a superuser with a court subpoena.

The ledgerHash-chained rows

Every action points to the one before it.

Each row in the custody ledger includes the SHA-256 of the previous row. To forge a single event, an attacker must recompute every subsequent row and re-collect every subsequent witness signature. The ledger becomes more expensive to fake with every passing hour.

The refusalWhy superusers can't help

We told PostgreSQL to refuse, and we meant it.

Row-level security policies on custody_events deny UPDATE and DELETE to every role — including postgres itself. The only way around it is to drop the table, which breaks the chain — defence counsel will see it, the clerk validator will see it, and the case will collapse at admissibility.

RLS

DB-enforced

Not app-enforced. The refusal lives below ORM, below the API layer, below any admin panel. Even psql cannot edit a sealed row.

WORM

Disk-enforced

Optional: back the tablespace with WORM-compliant storage (Cohesity, NetApp SnapLock). Now even a DROP TABLE requires physical intervention.

PROOF

Chain-enforced

If someone does drop and reconstruct: the chain's Merkle root diverges from every peer's copy. The defence's clerk validator reports the break in under a second.

ProofsWhat the validator checks

Every exhibit ships with its own proof of life.

When you export a case file, VaultKeeper bundles the evidence, the custody log, a timestamped hash manifest, and a 240-line Python validator. Defence counsel's clerk runs the validator offline. Either the chain holds, or it doesn't — no phone call to our support team required.

The claim

Row 48,217 cannot have been forged after 19 April 2026, 14:44:30 UTC.

The proof

The row's hash is bound to row 48,216, which was RFC-3161 timestamped by tsa.dfn.de. A forgery would require breaking SHA-256, the TSA's RSA key, and collecting post-hoc signatures from witnesses 02 and 03.
