01 · Evidence management

Upload a 10 GB drone capture from a field laptop on satellite.

Resumable chunked uploads over the tus protocol. The browser hashes every chunk with SHA-256 before it's sent; the server verifies on arrival and refuses with a 409 if a single byte is off. EXIF and metadata are extracted automatically. Versioning is a parent-child chain — no "overwrite", ever.

  • Resumable uploads up to 10 GB, auto-retry on drop
  • Client-side SHA-256 with Web Crypto API
  • EXIF, codec, and file-system metadata captured on ingest
  • Immutable version history; previews for image, PDF, audio, video
  • Classification levels: Public / Restricted / Confidential / Ex-Parte

02 · Chain of custody

An audit trail the database itself refuses to rewrite.

The custody log is enforced at the Postgres level with row-level security. Every entry carries the SHA-256 of the previous entry — a tamper-evident linked list that fails verification if anyone, including a root superuser, alters history. Advisory locks serialize concurrent writes; there are no race conditions to exploit.

  • Append-only, RLS-enforced; UPDATE and DELETE are statically impossible
  • Hash chaining across every row; a break is visible immediately
  • Every action logged — upload, view, share, classify, disclose, destroy, redact
  • Court-submittable PDF custody reports, one click
  • Formal verification of the engine in progress (TLA+ / Coq)
03 · Witness protection

Names that only the right people ever see.

Witness PII — full name, contact info, location — is encrypted at the application layer with AES-256-GCM, not just at rest. Defence counsel never sees cleartext; they see a pseudonym like W-0144. Protection levels, threat models, and safety profiles live alongside the witness record, all searchable without ever exposing the identity.

  • AES-256-GCM envelope encryption of full_name, contact_info, location
  • Defence view: pseudonyms only. Prosecution view: clear-text on signed access.
  • Standard / Protected / High-risk tiers with per-witness threat profiles
  • Duress passphrase (planned): yields a decoy vault, signals silently
  • Every access written to the custody chain, with reason and role

04 · Search & discovery

A search bar that respects the permission matrix.

Meilisearch indexes every exhibit, note, and witness record — with typo tolerance and faceted filters — but every query is scoped by role. Defence never surfaces witness identities. Observers never surface ex-parte material. The same query returns different, legally correct result sets depending on who is asking.

  • Typo-tolerant full-text search, sub-50ms p95 on 10M+ exhibits
  • Faceted filtering by case, classification, type, tag, date range
  • Role-scoped results — defence, prosecution, judge, observer, victim rep
  • Highlighting in snippets so you know why a result matched
  • Saved searches, shareable filter states, CSV export for subsets
05 · Live collaboration

Three investigators, one analysis note. No merge conflicts.

Analysis notes, assessments, inquiry logs, and case timelines are backed by Yjs CRDTs over WebSockets. Presence indicators show who is reading and who is typing. Network drops? The document reconciles itself when connectivity returns. Every save is a new version on the custody chain — so you can always trace who changed what, when.

  • Yjs CRDT — conflict-free concurrent editing, offline-first
  • Presence tracking — cursors, selections, avatars in the margin
  • Automatic reconnection; no lost edits on a dropped satellite link
  • Version history stored on the same custody chain as exhibits
  • Rich text + linked mentions to exhibits, witnesses, and other notes

06 · Redactions & disclosure

Defence gets the redacted copy. The original never moves.

Our redaction editor OCRs the page, lets you drag masks over names, accounts, and locations — and writes a new disclosure version alongside the original. Drafts aren't finalized until you explicitly say so. Disclosure packages bundle redacted versions with a manifest, signed and hash-chained.

  • Visual mask editor on top of OCR text layer
  • Side-by-side compare: original vs. each redacted version
  • Multi-item disclosure packages with full recipient tracking
  • Drafts, finalization states, and revocation paths
  • Every redaction written to the custody chain with reason
07 · Trusted timestamping

A time the court already trusts.

Every sealed exhibit gets a RFC 3161 timestamp from an external, qualified Time-Stamping Authority — the same legal instrument used in EU qualified e-signatures. Your case doesn't depend on VaultKeeper's clock. It depends on a certificate a European court already recognises.

  • RFC 3161 timestamps from eIDAS-registered TSAs
  • Optional secondary anchor to a public blockchain (OpenTimestamps)
  • Verifiable offline with our MIT-licensed clerk validator
  • Timestamps survive the company that issued them

08 · Ops & integration

The invisible half that keeps tribunals running.

Chain of custody is the part users see. Underneath sits the infrastructure that keeps an air-gapped deployment in Phnom Penh as happy as a ten-seat NGO in The Hague.

Auth · Keycloak

OIDC + SAML. Delegated MFA. System + case roles. 15-min access tokens, 8-hr refresh, 3 concurrent sessions.

Backups

Nightly encrypted Postgres dumps, MinIO snapshots to a separate Storage Box. RTO < 1 hr, RPO 24 hrs.

Health & alerts

Public /health. Admin dashboard covers Postgres, MinIO, Meilisearch, disk, last-backup age.

API keys

Case-scoped, read-only or read-write. Rate-limited. Every call written to the custody chain.

Multi-org

Each institution in its own org. Users span multiple. Hard data isolation at the DB row level.

Deployment

Docker Compose + Terraform + Ansible. Automated Hetzner provisioning in minutes.

Ingest connectors

Signed agents for X-Ways, Cellebrite UFED, Magnet AXIOM, Autopsy, OpenText EnCase.

On-prem AI

Whisper transcription, OCR, translation, entity extraction, semantic search. Nothing leaves the box.
