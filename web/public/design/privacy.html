Company · Privacy

We run software that protects witnesses. We had better take this seriously.

This policy covers the data VaultKeeper Coöperatief U.A. — the Dutch cooperative — collects about people who use our website, request pilots, or hold paid contracts with us. It does not cover the evidence data inside a customer's self-hosted VaultKeeper instance: we have no access to that, by architecture. This page is written by a human; a lawyer at Houthoff Amsterdam reviewed it on 2026-01-14.

1 · What we don't see

VaultKeeper customers run the software themselves. Evidence, witness identities, case metadata, AI pipeline results, and user activity inside those instances never leave the customer's perimeter. We cannot read them. No backup, no analytics, no "model training", no telemetry. If a hostile party sent us a subpoena for a tribunal's case file, our honest answer would be that we do not have it.

2 · What we do collect, about you

Only what we need to run a small website and schedule pilot calls:

  • Pilot request form. Your name, institution, jurisdiction, role, team size, and free-text response. Held in Proton Mail; retained for 24 months unless you ask us to delete it sooner.
  • Paid-customer contacts. Billing address, VAT number, and up to three technical contacts. Held in our accounting system (Moneybird, EU-hosted). Retained for seven years per Dutch fiscal law.
  • Support conversations. If you open a support thread, we retain the thread for 18 months. Ours is the only system it lives in.
  • Website logs. IP, user-agent, URL, timestamp. Retained 14 days, then discarded. No cookies; no trackers; no analytics vendor.

3 · What we will not do

We will not sell, rent, or share your data with marketers, brokers, analytics firms, or AI companies. We will not use contract data to train any model. We will not set advertising cookies. We will not deploy session-replay tooling. These are not forward-looking commitments — they are present-day refusals.

4 · Sub-processors

A short, auditable list:

  • Proton Mail — mail, calendar, shared drive. Swiss jurisdiction.
  • Moneybird — bookkeeping & invoicing. NL jurisdiction.
  • Hetzner — this website. DE jurisdiction. Logs retained 14 days.
  • Stripe — payment processing, EU entity only. IE jurisdiction.

We change sub-processors only by a board-approved resolution, posted publicly 30 days in advance. The full resolution log is linked from our legal page.

5 · Your rights under the GDPR

You may request access, rectification, erasure, restriction of processing, data portability, or withdrawal of consent at any time. Requests go to privacy@vaultkeeper.coop and are answered, in writing, within 14 days. We have never refused a request. You can also complain to the Autoriteit Persoonsgegevens (Dutch DPA); we will not retaliate.

6 · Transfers outside the EU

We do not transfer personal data outside the EU/EEA. If a sub-processor did operate non-EU infrastructure, we would switch. Our legal entity, staff, and infrastructure are all EU-resident.

7 · How we handle government requests

We receive lawful requests. We respond to them. We also publish a semi-annual transparency report disclosing the number of requests received, the jurisdiction, and the disposition (complied, challenged, refused). If we are ever compelled not to publish — a gag order — we will discontinue the "no gag received" canary on our legal page. Its absence is itself a notification. As of publication, the canary remains.
